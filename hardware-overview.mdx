## Variants

WiCAN is available in three hardware variants. Choose the one that matches your use case.

### WiCAN OBD-II

An ESP32-C3-based CAN bus adapter in an OBD-II plug form factor. Connects directly to the vehicle's OBD-II port and is powered from PIN 16 (battery voltage). Communicates over WiFi and BLE.

- ELM327 v1.5 emulation
- WiFi (AP and station mode) and BLE
- SocketCAN / slcan support
- RealDash support via "realdash 66" protocol
- MQTT telemetry and AutoPID
- Sleep mode (drops below 1 mA when triggered by low voltage)
- CAN2.0A/B up to 1 Mbit/s on PIN 6 (CANH) and PIN 14 (CANL)

### WiCAN USB

The USB form factor shares nearly all firmware features with WiCAN OBD-II but is packaged for bench and remote use rather than a vehicle port.

- Same ESP32-C3 hardware and firmware as WiCAN OBD-II
- **Extended voltage range up to 36 V** — suitable for 12 V and 24 V truck setups
- Powered via USB or screw-terminal connector
- Can operate as a USB-to-CAN adapter when WiFi is not needed
- Screw-terminal connector for CANH, CANL, power, and TR (termination)
- Diode protection on the USB port

### WiCAN PRO

WiCAN PRO uses a dedicated advanced OBD chip instead of a bare ESP32, which enables compatibility with a much wider range of vehicle protocols. It also adds USB host capability, an SD card slot, WireGuard VPN, HTTPS, MQTTS, and Nabu Casa support for Home Assistant.

<Note>
  WiCAN PRO is the recommended variant. The original WiCAN hardware has memory limitations that restrict future firmware updates. WiCAN PRO does not have this constraint.
</Note>

## OBD-II pinout (WiCAN OBD-II and WiCAN PRO)

The OBD-II connector uses the standard J1962 Type A pinout. WiCAN uses the following pins:

| PIN | Signal | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 4 | Chassis GND | Chassis ground |
| 5 | Signal GND | Signal ground |
| 6 | CANH | CAN bus high (standard high-speed CAN) |
| 14 | CANL | CAN bus low (standard high-speed CAN) |
| 16 | Battery+ | 12 V supply from vehicle battery |

WiCAN PRO also accesses additional pins for extended protocol support:

| PIN | Signal | Protocol |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | SW-CAN | Single Wire CAN (GMLAN) |
| 3 | MS-CANH | Medium speed CAN high |
| 11 | MS-CANL | Medium speed CAN low |
| 12 | GM-CANH | GM high-speed J1962 CAN high |
| 13 | GM-CANL | GM high-speed J1962 CAN low |

## USB pinout (WiCAN USB)

The WiCAN USB screw-terminal connector exposes:

| Pin | Signal | Description |
|---|---|---|
| CANH | CAN high | Connect to bus CANH |
| CANL | CAN low | Connect to bus CANL |
| TR | Termination | Connect to CANH to activate the 120 Ω termination resistor |
| 12V / GND | Power | External power supply (up to 36 V) |

<Note>
  To activate the built-in 120 Ω termination resistor, connect the **TR** pin to **CANH**. The other end of the resistor is connected to CANL internally. Use this when WiCAN USB is at either end of a CAN bus segment.
</Note>

## Supported CAN bit rates

Both WiCAN and WiCAN PRO support the following CAN bit rates on the standard CAN interface (PIN 6/14):

| Rate | Common use |
|---|---|
| 5 Kbit/s | Low-speed legacy bus |
| 10 Kbit/s | — |
| 20 Kbit/s | — |
| 25 Kbit/s | — |
| 50 Kbit/s | — |
| 100 Kbit/s | — |
| 125 Kbit/s | ISO 11898 default |
| 250 Kbit/s | SAE J1939 |
| 500 Kbit/s | Most modern vehicles |
| 800 Kbit/s | — |
| 1000 Kbit/s (1 Mbit/s) | High-speed automotive |

The CAN controller supports **CAN 2.0A** (11-bit IDs) and **CAN 2.0B** (29-bit IDs) frames at all rates up to 1 Mbit/s.

Set the bit rate in the **Settings** tab under **CAN Bitrate** before connecting to a bus.
